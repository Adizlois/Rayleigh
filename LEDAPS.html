5. LEDAPS (Landsat Ecosystem Disturbance Adaptive Processing System)



LEDAPS is a NASA-funded project that produces maps of surface reflectance derived from Landsat imagery to support a variety of ecosystem studies.
LEDAPS atmopheric correction process makes use of the dark, dense, vegetation (DDV) method of Kaufman et al. (Kaufman et al., 1997; Remer et al., 2005) in order to extract aerosol optical thickness (AOT) directly from the imagery. It works quite similar to our adaption of Wang's algorithm, but instead of clear water pixels, it takes inland vegetation pixels to determine aerosol radiance.
Based on the physical correlation between chlorophyll absorption and bound water absorption, DDV method postulates a linear relation between shortwave-infrared (2.2 μm) surface reflectance (nearly unaffected by the atmosphere) and surface reflectance in the visible bands. By using the relation to calculate surface reflectance for the visible bands, and comparing the result to the TOA reflectance, aerosol optical depth may be estimated. For LEDAPS AOT estimation, each image is averaged to 1 km resolution (to suppress local heterogeneity), and candidate "dark targets" with TOA are selected. Water targets are excluded and therefore DDV correction is not affected by turbidity.
